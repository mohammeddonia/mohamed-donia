Comparison of Noisy Gradient Algorithms in Big Data Bayesian Learning

Affiliation: This project is part of a course to complete an MSc degree in Computational Applied Mathematics (CAM) at the University of Edinburgh. Course: Research Skills for CAM.

Supervised by: Benedict Leimkuhler and Charles Matthew.

Done with: Katherine, Kostas and Jikai.

March 2019

Summary:
In big data, combining the gradient calculation of noisy minibatches of the data with the bayesian framework of statistical inference is an approach to tackle the infeasibility of the large scale gradient calculation and the inaccuracy coming from calculting the gradient with a small batch of the data. This project investigates the different noisy gradient methods for machine learning and pinpoints the differences.
